INTRODUCTION


The organization has given dedicated and relentless service to all types of sick and injured animals. Approximately 9000 to 10000 animals are treated in a year out of which 300 to 400 are always in-patients. Our organization is registered with the Charity Commissioner; Registration No. F-304 (BOM) dated 2nd June 1953 under the Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950.


The Royal Society of Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (UK) was founded on 16th June 1824 by the Rev Arthur Broome, Richard Martin and a few others humanitarians. At that time, the concept of animal welfare was ridiculed upon, brutality being commonplace. The only law for the protection of animals was Martin's Act passed in 1822. Though the first of its kind in the world, it only applied to horses and cattle. In 1835, Queen Victoria became a patron of the RSPCA and animals entering the society's portals have been mothered since. As news of this humanitarian work spread, country after country founded their respective SPCAs, all patterned on the RSPCA. Hence the BSPCA came into being and has continued its work towards animal welfare ever since.0



ACTIVITIES


The Bombay SPCA including the Bai Sakarbai Dinshaw Petit Hospital for Animals had organized the Animal Welfare Training Programme from 23rd February to 29th February 2004 on behalf of Animal Welfare Board of India for Animal Welfare Officers. There were 27 participants from other SPCA's, NGO's and individuals from all over Maharashtra State. The seven days course covered theory classes on various aspects on Prevention of Cruelty to Animals & Accounting procedures. The course was conducted by the various Presidents / Chief Executive Officers of NGO's and the legal classes by Advocates.

MISSION


  1. We firmly believe that no species can exist in isolation, not even man. Only when all life forms come together and exist in harmony can there be an ecological balance.

  2. We help contain pollutants caused by animals.

  3. We ensure that animals brought to Mumbai's slaughterhouse are transported, handled, and kept humanely, in a manner that complies with the law.

  4. We ensure that The Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act is enforced and animals are provided with food, water and shelter to conform to the required standards and that animals captured from the wild are returned to the forest areas.

  5. We keep a check on animals to see that they are kept in hygienic stables and are looked after properly; thus helping to make life easier for them and for the people who co-exist with them.
